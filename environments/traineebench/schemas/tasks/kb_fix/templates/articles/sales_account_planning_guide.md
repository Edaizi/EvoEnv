# Sales Account Planning Guide

Account planning is the discipline of understanding customers deeply and aligning resources to win, expand, and retain revenue efficiently.

## Research

- Org chart mapping: economic buyer, champions, blockers, influencers
- Business priorities: public filings, press, analyst notes
- Technology landscape: current stack, constraints, adjacent tools

## Strategy

- Value hypothesis: pains, desired outcomes, and impact model
- Competitive posture: replacement, displacement, or net-new budget
- Entry points: pilot use cases and success criteria

## Plan

- 3x3 plan: three stakeholders Ã— three actions each
- Timeline: proof of value, executive readout, commercial close
- Risks & mitigations: legal timing, security reviews, budget cycles

## Execution

- Multi-threading cadence with champions and economic buyer
- Co-create business case; quantify ROI and risk reduction
- Objection handling playbook; reference calls and trials

## Expansion & Retention

- Success plan with customer success and product adoption goals
- QBR rhythm; roadmap alignment; measurable outcomes
- Early warning signals: low usage, support escalations, executive churn

## Metrics

- Win rate by segment; cycle length; average selling price
- Expansion rate; gross/dollar net retention; multi-year adoption
- Forecast accuracy; pipeline hygiene score

## Conclusion

Deliberate account planning shifts outcomes from reactive to predictable. Write it down, revisit it, and improve it every quarter.
